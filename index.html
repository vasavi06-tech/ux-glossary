UX Glossary

Learn key UX terms, one card at a time ✨

Persona

A fictional character based on user research representing a user type that might use your product.

Empathy Map

A collaborative tool used to understand the user's feelings, thoughts, and behaviors.

Wireframe

A visual guide that represents the skeletal framework of a digital product or website.

User Flow

The path taken by a user to complete a specific task on a website or app.

Usability

The ease with which users can use a product to achieve their goals effectively and efficiently.

Accessibility

Designing products so that people with disabilities can use them equally and independently.

Heuristic Evaluation

A usability inspection method where experts evaluate a UI based on established usability principles.

Information Architecture

The organization and structure of content in a way that users can easily navigate and find what they need.

User Research

The process of studying users' needs and behaviors to inform design decisions.

Prototype

A basic version of a product used to test design concepts and gather feedback before development.

CTA (Call to Action)

A prompt (like a button or link) that encourages users to take a specific action, such as “Sign Up” or “Buy Now.”

A/B Testing

A method where two versions of a design are tested to see which performs better with users.

Fidelity (Low/High)

The level of detail in a prototype. Low-fidelity is basic (sketch), high-fidelity looks like the final product.
